Pipeline Architecture

From TIFF to Prediction
in 6 steps

📁
Step 01
Upload Hyperspectral TIFF
User uploads a .tif / .tiff hyperspectral image. The platform validates format, extracts metadata, and confirms 204 spectral bands are present.
st.file_uploader(type=["tif","tiff"])
⚙️
Step 02
Preprocessing
Band extraction, per-band normalization (min-max scaling), spatial mean pooling, and removal of water absorption bands at 1400nm and 1900nm.
preprocessing.py → normalize()
🔵
Step 03
PCA Dimensionality Reduction
204 spectral bands reduced to 50 principal components using sklearn PCA. Preserves ≥95% of variance while dramatically reducing input dimensions.
pca_processing.py → PCA(n_components=50)
🧠
Step 04
CNN Inference
PyTorch NitrogenCNN processes the 50-dim feature vector through convolutional blocks and outputs a scalar prediction score between 0 (sufficient) and 1 (deficient).
NitrogenCNN → sigmoid(output)
🔥
Step 05
Grad-CAM Explainability
Gradient-weighted class activation maps highlight which spectral regions and spatial zones drove the model's decision — making AI interpretable for agronomists.
GradCAM(model, target_layer)
📊
Step 06
Dashboard & History
Results displayed with confidence gauge, stored in SQLite, visualized in analytics dashboard. Export-ready for research reporting.
SQLite → predictions.db

Explainable AI

Why the model
made this decision

Grad-CAM overlays show exactly which leaf regions triggered the nitrogen deficiency prediction — turning a black box into a tool agronomists can trust.

🔴
High Activation Zones
Red/orange regions correspond to chlorophyll absorption bands (680nm) where nitrogen-stressed leaves show anomalous reflectance.
🟡
Medium Activation — NIR Region
Near-infrared (800–900nm) yellow zones indicate cell structure changes from nitrogen stress affecting mesophyll integrity.
🔵
Low Activation — Background
Cool blue areas contribute minimally to the prediction. Model correctly ignores soil background and imaging artifacts.
